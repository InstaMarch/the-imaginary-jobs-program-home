The Imaginary Jobs Program

Imagine real work

  • Voters lack confidence.
  • Communication is failing.
  • Most jobs seem meaningless.
  • Problems need solving.

We have made jobs to solve these problems:

  • Street activism
  • Non-partisan information to inform voters
  • Social media
  • Media making
  • Computer Programming

Agreements

  1. We limit our work to peaceful work.
  2. We avoid conflict with the law.
  3. We strive to only make welcome contact with others.
  4. We create opportunities to be pulled.
  5. We strive to provide services that are wanted.
  6. All media we generate goes to the Public Domain.
  7. We share and compare evidence in decision making.
  8. Shaming or humiliating are not our way.
  9. We do not subject volunteers to work they do not believe in.
  10. Prioritizing the work personally believed to be most meaningful ahead of the work believed to be less meaningful as part of a contemporary solution for morale problems.
  11. Our work is not-for-profit.
  12. We limit ourselves to respectful and kind behavior towards all life.
  13. We are a non-partisan organization that mediates contexts for diverse perspectives and open dialogue.

Your first imaginary job:

Currently we are taking applications for the Informed Citizens Alliance job of Election Information Researcher where we are creating non-partisan information about the candidates for elections wherever our volunteers are.

This includes building a datasource for:

  • Each candidate on each ballot
  • Each candidate's positions
  • Evidence for each position
  • Evidence against each position
  • Each candidate's voting record
  • Each candidate's financing

The job can be done from anywhere in the world that has a computer with an internet connection that can push to Github (almost all can).
